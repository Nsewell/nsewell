Tony Abbott Messes With ACT's Marriage Laws

Oct 11th, 2013

When Tony Abbott was asked about his views on same sex marriage in the leader’s debate, he replied with this:

“It’s a very important issue. I know how important an issue it is. It’s not the only important issue and I’ve got to say as far as an incoming Coalition government is concerned, the priority will be on things like reducing cost-of-living pressure and increasing job security.”

That must have been a “non-core” promise, given that George Brandis is almost straight away working to overrule a law on same sex marriage the ACT wants to bring in. George Brandis has claimed that the ACT Bill would undermine the ‘well-established position’ of ‘nationally consistent marriage laws.’ The Tasmanian Law Reform commission suggests that this is possibly not the case, with a history of states passing their own marriage laws, so it’s unknown if the ACT bill will be found constitutional. Politifact rates Brandis’s statement as half true.

While Abbott noted in the debate that same sex marriage was an “important” issue for his lesbian sister, his view on the issue has not “evolved” but goes against the majority of the Australian population. Figures from the ABC’s Vote Compass survey of 422,000 respondents show that the majority of Australians support same sex marriage, and 70% of those under 34 do. This is in line with previous opinion polls finding that about 60% of the country now supports same sex marriage.

There’s an old quote “every time you hear an ambulance siren, an opponent of gay rights is leaving the world or a supporter is entering it”. In reality, with NZ and the UK passing gay marriage laws it’s already a lost argument. The UK law will come into effect in 2014, I don’t think the Christian lobby can hold the issue back that much longer.
