You Can Heal Cancer Naturally With Coffee Enemas, but Only if They're Organic

Jun 24th, 2013

No, wait, you can’t. Jessica Ainscough, a self proclaimed “wellness warrior” has recently complained that she felt “dirty” after eating a vegan burger that wasn’t 100% organic. The burger was billed by the not-for-profit vegetarian cafe as “cancer fighting rather than cancer causing.” Ainscough later rang the cafe to check up on some of their claims, but not whether any of the claimed miraculous healing properties had any empirical basis, but rather if their food was 100% organic. The revelation that her burger and juice weren’t purely organic made her feel like such a dirty, dirty woman that she needed detoxifying:

I felt dirty. I felt like I needed to give my insides about 10 showers. I was so shocked that I just hung up the phone, but I wish I’d told her that her since her “cancer fighting” burger contains pesticides it is actually cancer causing.

Emphasis hers. The poor woman is dying of a rare type of cancer, epithelioid sarcoma, which tends to show up in young patients. Unfortunately, with no paletable treatment options available, the woman has chosen to believe in the claimed “natural healing power” of miracle diets to avoid acknowledging her fate. The yellow journalism of the Daily Tele repeats this bunk untramelled:

The holistic health coach, who was in Byron Bay last week, follows a strict organic diet after overcoming cancer without using modern medicine.

This research led me to Gerson Therapy which ensures you have a perfectly balanced diet for optimum health, assisting your body to flush out nasties whilst feeding it with all the goodness it needs to flourish.

The therapy involves drinking 13 fresh organic veggie juices per day (yes that’s one an hour, every hour of my waking day), five coffee enemas per day and a basic organic whole food plant-based diet with additional supplements.

The Gerson Therapy has been extensively debunked, but is unfortunately continuing to exploit desperate people.

She goes on to invent ”psychic pollution”

Did you know that pollution on our planet is not only caused by greenhouse gases, littering and chemical waste but also by our negative thoughts? Yes, you did read that last sentence correctly; our negative thoughts also contribute to pollution on our planet. This is called ‘psychic pollution.’ This fact has been proven: what we think and feel has a direct impact on the world around us.
