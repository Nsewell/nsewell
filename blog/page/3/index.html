I recently came across a talk by Professor Gareth Williams on the history of the anti-vaccine movement, going right back to Jenner himself. Some doctors opposed cowpox vaccination as smallpox cases were a large portion of their business, but most interestingly many people found religious arguments against vaccination. Theologians in France and England argued that ”diseases are sent by Providence for the punishment of sin” and suggested that Job’s boils and ulcers were a result of cowpox vaccination by Satan. One of the most wacko opponents of the Jennerian society wrote that smallpox helped to lower the size of poor families:

why is it not remembered that in the populous parts of the Metropolis, where the abundance of children exceed the means of providing food, and raiment for them, this pestilential disease is considered as a merciful provision on the part of Providence, to lessen the burthen of a poor man’s family ?
