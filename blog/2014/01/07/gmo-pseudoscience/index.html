A Lonely Quest for Facts on Genetically Modified Crops

Jan 7th, 2014

There’s a great article in the New York Times about how politicians and the public in Hawaii are refusing to listen to scientists about how Genetically Modified organisms are safe to eat. It links to a great graph from boingboing pointing out the fallacy of confusing corellation with causation:

Science Magazine is one of the bastions of reason, with Nobel lauriets signing an open letter denouncing the destruction of test crops

New technologies often evoke rumors of hazard. These generally fade with time when, as in this case, no real hazards emerge. But the anti-GMO fever still burns brightly, fanned by electronic gossip and well-organized fear-mongering that profits some individuals and organizations. We, and the thousands of other scientists who have signed the statement of protest, stand together in staunch opposition to the violent destruction of required tests on valuable advances such as Golden Rice that have the potential to save millions of impoverished fellow humans from needless suffering and death.

Others, such as the co-Founder of Greenpeace, Dr. Patrick Moore, have spoken out against the group’s increasingly hysterical opposition to GM crops like Golden Rice:
