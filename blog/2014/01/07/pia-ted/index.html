Pia Waugh on Technology's New Citizinship at TEDx

Jan 7th, 2014

Pia Waugh is the former president of Linux Australia, and she gave a great talk at a TEDx event in Canberra:

The new citizenship: Pia Waugh at TEDxCanberraWomen
Pia Waugh is an open government and open data advocate, working to enable greater transparency, democratic engagement, citizen-centric design, and pragmatic innovation in the public sector and beyond. She believes that tech culture has a huge role to play in achieving better policy planning, outcomes, public engagement and a better public service. In the spirit of ideas worth spreading, TEDx is a program of local, self-organized events that bring people together to share a TED-like experience. At a TEDx event, TEDTalks video and live speakers combine to spark deep discussion and connection in a small group. These local, self-organized events are branded TEDx, where x = independently organized TED event. The TED Conference provides general guidance for the TEDx program, but individual TEDx events are self-organized.* (*Subject to certain rules and regulations)

A few great points * Inverting William Gibson’s view: “The future is here, and it is widely distributed” * There is a global shift in power from the West to the East, and from traditional centralised power structures to users. * The ability to see different perspectives over the same medium challenges the idea of a single source of truth. * The freedom of the users comes directly from the freedom of the tools they use. * Technologists have the abilitiy to route around a damaged system, but they have a moral responsibility to represent those that can’t. * Internet users are among only a third of the world, and so every internet user has the power to effect the world in positive ways. * Pia on Hipsters: Billie Brag quote “Cynicism is the perfect enemy of progress.” * If we don’t work to shape the future, someone else is going to shape it for us. * The future is not just being determned in traditional ways.
