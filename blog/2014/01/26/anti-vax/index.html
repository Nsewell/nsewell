New Map Shows the Harm of the Anti-Vaccine Movement

Jan 26th, 2014

A new map by the Council on Foreign Relations shows the rising number of vaccine preventable diseases in first world contries. After Andrew Wakefield took out a patent on a single Measels vaccine he falsified a study that appeared to show that appeared to show that the Measels, Mumps and Rubella triple antigen was unsafe for children, proposing his own patented vaccine as a “safer” alternative. Even after Wakefield’s paper has been retracted for innacuracy, and he was struck off the medical register for “dishonest”, “unethical” and “callous” behaviour, anti-vaccine hysteria continues to spread thoughout the world. The drop in MMR vaccination rates in Wales to below 80% has caused a widespread measels outbreak, leaving parents of infected teenagers feeling betrayed by the lies of the anti-vaccine movement:

Wakefield MMR fraud comes home to roost in Wales
Australian ABC Lateline programme looks at the early-2013 outbreak of measles in Wales UK. A recent measles fatality is likely linked to the Andrew Wakefield MMR fraud which led to a baseless reduction in uptake of the MMR vaccine. It’s apparent that now fear of the demonstrable harm of measles outweighs the fallacious fear of MMR. Notifications equal 800 since November 2012 - approximately 7 times that for the entire year of 2012. The vast majority of parents now accept that the “callous” and deregistered Wakefield is a charlatan and a fraud.

Interestingly, Wakefield is only the latest in a long line of cranks opposed to the vaccination of children. I recently came across a talk by Professor Gareth Williams on the history of the anti-vaccine movement, going right back to Jenner himself. Some doctors opposed cowpox vaccination as smallpox cases were a large portion of their business, but most interestingly many people found religious arguments against vaccination. Theologians in France and England argued that ”diseases are sent by Providence for the punishment of sin” and suggested that Job’s boils and ulcers were a result of cowpox vaccination by Satan. One of the most wacko opponents of the Jennerian society wrote that smallpox helped to lower the size of poor families:

why is it not remembered that in the populous parts of the Metropolis, where the abundance of children exceed the means of providing food, and raiment for them, this pestilential disease is considered as a merciful provision on the part of Providence, to lessen the burthen of a poor man’s family ?

Interestingly, while most doctors (Wakefield excluded) would never seek to harm their patients to increase their own business, many pastors coninue to teach that children should not be vaccinated against infectious diseases. Measels outbreaks have been observed in areas with low vaccination rates, commonly correlated with high numbers of evangelical megachurches. Unfortunately, a number of churches order their congregation to eschew medical treatment in favour of prayer, which Republican lawmakers protect as “religious freedom”, even after a spate of preventable deaths.
